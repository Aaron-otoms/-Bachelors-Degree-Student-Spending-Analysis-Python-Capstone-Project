# Bachelor's Degree Student Spending Analysis — Python Capstone Project
## 📌 Overview
This project analyzes the spending habits of 1,000 university students using Python. The dataset covers demographic, financial, and academic information, allowing for a deep exploration of how students across different majors, age groups, year levels, and genders manage their income and expenses.
## 🎯 Business Problem
Which student groups are most financially strained, what are the major cost drivers, and how do spending patterns vary across majors, age groups, and year levels?
## 📊 Dataset
- Source: Kaggle — Students Spending Habits dataset
- Size: 1,000 rows × 17 columns
- Key fields: Age, Gender, Year in School, Major, Monthly Income, Financial Aid, Tuition, Housing, Food, Transportation, Entertainment, Personal Care, Technology, Health & Wellness, Miscellaneous, Preferred Payment Method
## 🛠️ Tools Used
- Python (Pandas) — data cleaning, feature engineering, and exploratory data analysis
- Jupyter Notebook — analysis environment
## 🔍 Approach
Imported the dataset using Pandas and performed an initial audit
Removed duplicates and standardized column names
Handled missing values using mean imputation
Engineered 7 new columns for richer financial analysis
Conducted EDA across majors, age groups, year levels, and gender
Performed correlation analysis between financial aid and monthly income
## 💡 Key Findings
- Psychology students pay the highest average tuition fees; Engineering students pay the least
- Biology students incur the highest total non-tuition expenses
- Students aged 20-22 incur the most expenses overall
- Freshmen have the highest average income; Sophomores have the lowest
- Engineering major students have the highest average total income
- Senior year students and those aged 23-25 are the most financially strained
- Male students are more likely to be financially strained
- The most preferred payment method is Mobile App, followed by Credit/Debit Card
